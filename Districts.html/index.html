Discover Andhra Pradesh

Andhra Pradesh is a state on the southeastern coast of India, known for its rich cultural heritage, natural beauty, and historical significance. It is the seventh-largest state in India by area and the tenth-most populous. Telugu, a classical language of India, is the official and most widely spoken language.

The capital city is Amaravati, while Visakhapatnam is the largest city and a major port. Andhra Pradesh shares borders with Telangana, Chhattisgarh, Odisha, Karnataka, and Tamil Nadu, and has the longest coastline in India along the Bay of Bengal.

The state has a deep historical background, with archaeological evidence suggesting human habitation for over 2,47,000 years. Dynasties such as the Satavahanas, Eastern Chalukyas, Kakatiyas, Vijayanagara Empire, and Qutb Shahis have ruled this land, contributing to its cultural and architectural heritage. The region was later part of British India before becoming a separate state in 1953, and in 2014, Telangana was carved out of it.

Andhra Pradesh is also known for its vibrant festivals, traditional dance forms like Kuchipudi, delicious cuisine, and thriving agriculture. With modern cities, historical monuments, and beautiful beaches, it offers a blend of tradition and progress.
